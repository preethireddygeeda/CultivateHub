Lady's Finger (Okra) Cultivation

Lady's Finger (Okra)

Climate: Thrives in warm, tropical climates with temperatures between 25°C to 35°C.

Seed Requirement: Approximately 10-12 kg per hectare.

Land Preparation: Prepare the land by plowing 2-3 times to create a fine tilth. Incorporate organic matter or compost to enrich the soil.

Planting: Seeds can be sown directly in the field or started in nurseries. Plant spacing should be around 30-45 cm between rows and 20-30 cm between plants.

Fertilizers: Use a balanced fertilizer regimen. A typical recommendation is N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 30:30:30 kg/ha. Apply organic manure to enhance soil fertility.

Water Supply: Requires regular irrigation, especially during dry periods. Ensure adequate moisture in the soil, avoiding waterlogging.

Disease Management: Common diseases include powdery mildew and root rot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply fungicides if necessary. Proper spacing and sanitation are essential to prevent disease spread.
