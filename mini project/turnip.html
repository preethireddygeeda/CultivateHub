Turnip Leaves

           

Climate: Turnips thrive in cool weather, with optimal temperatures between 10°C to 24°C. They are typically grown in spring and fall.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 2-3 kg of seeds per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and fertile. Prepare the land by plowing and incorporating organic matter or compost. The ideal soil pH is between 5.5 and 6.8.

           

Planting: Seeds can be sown directly into the field. Plant seeds 1 cm deep and space rows 30-45 cm apart. Thin seedlings to 15-20 cm apart once they are large enough to handle.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer, such as N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 60:40:40 kg/ha. Additional nitrogen may be needed during the growing period.

           

Water Supply: Maintain consistent moisture throughout the growing season. Water regularly to keep the soil moist but avoid waterlogging, which can lead to root diseases.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include downy mildew, clubroot, and leaf spot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Ensure proper spacing and avoid overhead irrigation to minimize disease spread.
