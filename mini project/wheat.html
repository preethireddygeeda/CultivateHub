Wheat Cultivation

       
               
           

Wheat

           

Climate: Wheat thrives in cool and dry climates. The ideal temperature for wheat growth is between 10°C to 25°C. It requires well-distributed rainfall of 500-1000 mm during its growing season.

           

Seed Requirement: The seed rate is about 100-125 kg per hectare for normal sowing and 125-150 kg per hectare for late sowing.

           

Land Preparation: The land should be plowed thoroughly and leveled. It is essential to have a well-prepared seedbed that is free from weeds and has good tilth. Application of organic matter or compost helps in improving soil fertility.

           

Planting: Sowing time varies with the region. In India, wheat is generally sown from late October to December. Seeds are sown at a depth of 4-5 cm with a spacing of 20-22.5 cm between rows.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced dose of N:P:K fertilizers. A typical recommendation is 120 kg of Nitrogen, 60 kg of Phosphorus, and 40 kg of Potassium per hectare. Nitrogen is usually applied in two or three splits.

           

Water Supply: Wheat requires about 4-6 irrigations depending on the soil type and weather conditions. Critical stages for irrigation are crown root initiation, tillering, jointing, heading, and grain filling.

           

Disease Management: Common wheat diseases include rusts, smuts, and powdery mildew. Use resistant varieties, follow crop rotation, and apply fungicides as necessary. Proper field sanitation and timely sowing help in managing diseases.
