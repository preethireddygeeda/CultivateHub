Sugarcane Cultivation

       
               
           

Sugarcane

           

Climate: Sugarcane thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with temperatures ranging from 20°C to 40°C. It requires a long, warm growing season with plenty of sunlight and moderate to high rainfall.

           

Seed Requirement: The seed requirement for sugarcane is about 8-10 tonnes per hectare. Healthy and disease-free setts (stem cuttings) with 2-3 buds each are used for planting.

           

Land Preparation: The land should be plowed deeply and thoroughly to a depth of 25-30 cm. It should be leveled and well-drained. Adding organic matter or compost can improve soil fertility and structure.

           

Planting: Sugarcane is typically planted in rows. Setts are planted either in furrows or trenches at a spacing of 90-150 cm between rows and 30-45 cm between setts. The planting depth is around 10-15 cm.

           

Fertilizers: Sugarcane is a heavy feeder and requires a balanced application of nutrients. The typical fertilizer recommendation is 150-200 kg of nitrogen, 60-80 kg of phosphorus, and 60-80 kg of potassium per hectare. Additional micronutrients may be required based on soil tests.

           

Water Supply: Sugarcane requires a consistent and adequate water supply. It needs about 1500-2500 mm of water during the growing season. Irrigation should be scheduled based on the crop's growth stage and soil moisture levels. Avoid waterlogging, as it can harm the crop.

           

Disease Management: Common sugarcane diseases include red rot, smut, and leaf scald. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Maintain field hygiene and proper spacing to reduce disease incidence.
