Fenugreek Leaves

           

Climate: Fenugreek is a cool-season crop and grows best in temperatures between 10°C and 30°C. It can tolerate light frost.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 20-25 kg per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and loamy. It needs to be plowed and harrowed to a fine tilth. Adding well-decomposed compost or farmyard manure enhances soil fertility.

           

Planting: Seeds are sown directly into the field. The spacing should be 20-25 cm between rows and 10-15 cm between plants. Seeds should be sown at a depth of 1-2 cm.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a basal dose of N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 20:40:20 kg/ha. Additional nitrogen may be required during the vegetative growth phase.

           

Water Supply: Fenugreek requires regular watering, especially during the initial stages of growth and dry periods. Avoid waterlogging to prevent root diseases.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include powdery mildew and root rot. Use disease-resistant varieties, ensure proper spacing for good air circulation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed.
