Brinjal (Eggplant) Cultivation

Brinjal

Climate: Thrives in warm weather, ideal temperatures range from 20°C to 30°C.

Seed Requirement: Approximately 250-300 grams of seeds per hectare.

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and loamy. Plow the land thoroughly and incorporate organic matter or compost. Soil pH should be around 5.5 to 7.0.

Planting: Seeds are usually sown in seedbeds and transplanted after 5-6 weeks. Maintain a spacing of 60-75 cm between rows and 30-45 cm between plants.

Fertilizers: A balanced application is essential. A typical recommendation is to apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 100:50:50 kg/ha. Additional nitrogen can be given during growth stages.

Water Supply: Regular irrigation is critical, especially during dry spells. Maintain consistent soil moisture, avoiding waterlogging which can lead to root rot.

Disease Management: Common diseases include bacterial wilt, powdery mildew, and fruit rot. Use resistant varieties, implement crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides or pesticides when necessary.
