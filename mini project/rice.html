Rice Plant Cultivation

       
               
           

Rice Plant

           

Climate: Rice thrives in warm, humid climates. Ideal temperatures range from 20°C to 30°C. It requires ample sunlight and is typically grown during the monsoon season.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 20-30 kg of seeds are required per hectare, depending on the variety and planting method.

           

Land Preparation: The land should be plowed 2-3 times to create a fine seedbed. Puddling (wet tillage) is essential for proper water retention. Leveling the field ensures uniform water distribution.

           

Planting: Seeds can be sown directly in the field or grown in nurseries and transplanted. For direct sowing, maintain a row spacing of 20-30 cm. For transplanting, seedlings should be 25-30 days old and planted 20 cm apart.

           

Fertilizers: A balanced fertilizer regimen is recommended, typically using N:P:K in a ratio of 80:40:40 kg/ha. Fertilizers should be applied at planting and during key growth stages.

           

Water Supply: Rice is a water-intensive crop and requires standing water in the fields during the growing season. Regular irrigation is necessary, particularly during dry spells.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include blast, blight, and leaf spot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides when needed. Maintain proper field sanitation to reduce disease incidence.
