Green Cabbage Cultivation

Green Cabbage

Climate: Prefers a cool climate with optimal temperatures between 15°C to 21°C.

Seed Requirement: Approximately 400-500 grams per hectare.

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and loamy. It needs to be plowed thoroughly and enriched with organic matter or compost. The ideal soil pH is between 6.0 and 6.5.

Planting: Seeds are sown in seedbeds and then transplanted to the main field after 4-6 weeks. Plant spacing should be around 45-60 cm between rows and 30-45 cm between plants.

Fertilizers: A balanced fertilizer regime is necessary. A typical recommendation is to apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 60:40:40 kg/ha. Additional nitrogen may be applied during the growing period.

Water Supply: Cabbage requires consistent moisture. Irrigate regularly to keep the soil evenly moist, particularly during head formation. Avoid waterlogging which can lead to root diseases.

Disease Management: Common diseases include black rot, downy mildew, and clubroot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Ensure proper spacing and avoid overhead irrigation to minimize disease spread.
