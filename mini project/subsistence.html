Subsistence Farming

Subsistence farming is a type of agriculture where farmers grow food primarily to feed themselves and their families. It is typically small-scale and relies on traditional methods and tools.

Why is Subsistence Farming Used?

  • Self-Sufficiency: Provides food for the farmer's family without relying on external markets.
  • Low Capital Requirement: Requires minimal financial investment and is accessible to low-income farmers.
  • Traditional Practices: Maintains cultural and agricultural traditions passed down through generations.

Importance of Subsistence Farming

  • Food Security: Ensures a stable food supply for the farmer's household.
  • Cultural Preservation: Keeps traditional farming methods and knowledge alive.
  • Local Economy: Supports the local economy by reducing dependence on external markets.

Advantages of Subsistence Farming

  • Independence: Farmers are not reliant on fluctuating market prices for their livelihood.
  • Environmental Sustainability: Often employs sustainable farming practices that maintain soil fertility and biodiversity.
  • Community Building: Strengthens local communities through shared agricultural practices and resources.
