Watermelon Cultivation

       
               
           

Watermelon

           

Climate: Watermelon requires a warm, sunny climate. Optimal temperatures for growth range from 25°C to 30°C.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 1.5-2.0 kg per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and sandy loam is ideal. Plow the field thoroughly and prepare raised beds to facilitate drainage. Incorporate organic matter or compost to enrich the soil. The ideal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.5.

           

Planting: Seeds are sown directly in the field. Plant spacing should be around 90-120 cm between rows and 60-90 cm between plants. Ensure good soil contact and moisture for germination.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer. A typical recommendation is to apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 60:40:40 kg/ha. Additional potassium and phosphorus may be required during the fruiting stage.

           

Water Supply: Watermelon requires consistent and adequate moisture, especially during flowering and fruit development. Drip irrigation is preferred to maintain uniform soil moisture. Avoid overwatering which can lead to root diseases.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include powdery mildew, downy mildew, and fusarium wilt. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Ensure proper spacing and adequate ventilation to minimize disease spread.
