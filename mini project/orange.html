Orange Cultivation

       
               
           

Orange

           

Climate: Oranges thrive in subtropical to tropical climates with temperatures between 13°C and 37°C. They require plenty of sunlight and moderate rainfall.

           

Seed Requirement: Seed propagation is not common for commercial cultivation; instead, grafting or budding methods are used. If seeds are used, about 10-15 seeds are required per square meter of nursery bed.

           

Land Preparation: The land should be thoroughly plowed and leveled. Well-drained sandy loam soil with a pH of 5.5 to 7.5 is ideal. Incorporate organic matter to improve soil fertility.

           

Planting: Planting is usually done in pits of 60x60x60 cm, spaced at 6-8 meters apart. The best time for planting is during the monsoon or early winter.

           

Fertilizers: Oranges require a balanced nutrient supply. A general recommendation is to apply 600-800 grams of nitrogen, 400-600 grams of phosphorus, and 600-800 grams of potassium per tree per year. Micronutrients like zinc and manganese are also important.

           

Water Supply: Regular watering is essential, especially during dry spells. Drip irrigation is recommended to ensure uniform moisture levels without waterlogging.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include citrus canker, gummosis, and root rot. Use disease-resistant rootstocks, practice good sanitation, and apply appropriate fungicides and bactericides. Ensure proper drainage and avoid over-watering to prevent root diseases.
