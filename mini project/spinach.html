Spinach Cultivation

       
               
           

Spinach

           

Climate: Spinach grows best in cool weather. Optimal temperatures for spinach growth range between 10°C to 20°C.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 10-12 kg of seeds per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: Spinach prefers well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter. The soil should be plowed and leveled properly. The ideal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.0.

           

Planting: Seeds can be sown directly into the field. Sow seeds in rows spaced 20-30 cm apart with a seed depth of 1-1.5 cm. Thin seedlings to 5-10 cm apart within rows.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer before planting. A typical recommendation is to apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 50:40:40 kg/ha. Additional nitrogen may be applied as a top-dressing during the growing period.

           

Water Supply: Spinach requires regular watering to keep the soil moist. Ensure consistent moisture, especially during germination and early growth stages. Avoid waterlogging which can lead to root diseases.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include downy mildew, leaf spots, and damping-off. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Ensure proper spacing and avoid overhead irrigation to minimize disease spread.
