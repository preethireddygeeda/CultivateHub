Coffee Cultivation

       
               
           

Coffee

           

Climate: Coffee thrives in tropical climates with temperatures ranging between 15°C to 24°C. It requires a high altitude of 600-2000 meters above sea level and prefers areas with high humidity and well-distributed rainfall.

           

Seed Requirement: High-quality seeds are essential. Typically, 1 kilogram of seeds is required per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: The land should be cleared of weeds and debris. Deep plowing is necessary to loosen the soil. Coffee plants prefer well-drained, fertile soils rich in organic matter with a pH range of 6.0 to 6.5.

           

Planting: Seeds are usually sown in nurseries and transplanted to the main field when they are 6-12 months old. The recommended spacing is 2-3 meters between rows and 1.5-2 meters between plants.

           

Fertilizers: Coffee plants require a balanced supply of nutrients. A typical recommendation is to apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 60:30:60 kg/ha. Organic manure and compost can also be used to enhance soil fertility.

           

Water Supply: Regular watering is crucial, especially during dry periods. Drip irrigation is recommended to provide a consistent supply of moisture and to prevent waterlogging.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include coffee leaf rust, coffee berry disease, and root rot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice proper spacing to improve air circulation, and apply appropriate fungicides as needed. Regular monitoring and good agricultural practices are essential to manage pests and diseases effectively.
