Coriander Cultivation

       
               
           

Coriander

           

Climate: Coriander grows well in cool and dry climates. The ideal temperature range is between 17°C to 27°C. It does not tolerate extreme heat or frost.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 20-25 kg of seeds per hectare. The seeds should be sown directly in the field.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and loamy, rich in organic matter. Plow the field thoroughly to ensure fine tilth. The soil pH should be between 6.0 and 8.0.

           

Planting: Seeds are sown directly in rows spaced 30 cm apart, with a spacing of 10-15 cm between plants. Sowing can be done from October to November for the best yield.

           

Fertilizers: Apply well-rotted farmyard manure or compost at the rate of 20-25 tons per hectare before sowing. Additionally, apply N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 30:20:10 kg/ha during the growing period.

           

Water Supply: Coriander requires moderate watering. Irrigate the field immediately after sowing and then at intervals of 7-10 days, depending on soil moisture and weather conditions. Avoid waterlogging.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include powdery mildew, wilt, and stem gall. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides. Ensure proper spacing and good air circulation to minimize disease occurrence.
