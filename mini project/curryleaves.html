Curry Leaf Cultivation

       
               
           

Curry Leaf

           

Climate: Prefers a tropical to subtropical climate. It grows well in temperatures between 16°C to 37°C.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 2.5 to 3 kg of seeds per hectare. Seeds should be fresh and sown immediately after extraction.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and fertile with good organic matter content. Plow the land thoroughly and create raised beds or ridges for better drainage.

           

Planting: Seeds are sown directly or seedlings are transplanted. For direct sowing, place seeds 1-2 cm deep in rows spaced 30-45 cm apart. Transplanting is done when seedlings are 4-6 weeks old.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer regime. Incorporate well-decomposed organic manure or compost into the soil. Chemical fertilizers like N:P:K in the ratio of 20:10:10 grams per plant can be applied.

           

Water Supply: Curry leaf plants require regular watering, especially during the initial stages of growth. Ensure the soil remains moist but not waterlogged. Reduce watering frequency during the winter season.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include leaf spot and powdery mildew. Practice crop rotation, ensure proper spacing, and avoid waterlogging. Use fungicides as needed and remove infected plant parts to control the spread of diseases.
