Dry Farming

Dry farming is an agricultural technique used in arid and semi-arid regions where water is scarce. It focuses on conserving soil moisture and growing drought-resistant crops.

Why is Dry Farming Used?

  • Water Conservation: Techniques are designed to maximize the use of limited water resources.
  • Drought Conditions: Provides solutions for farming in regions with low and unpredictable rainfall.
  • Crop Selection: Uses drought-resistant crops and methods to maintain productivity.

Importance of Dry Farming

  • Sustainability: Adapts farming practices to challenging climatic conditions for long-term productivity.
  • Resource Management: Efficiently manages water and soil resources in dry environments.
  • Food Security: Ensures crop production in regions with limited water availability.

Advantages of Dry Farming

  • Water Efficiency: Utilizes techniques to conserve soil moisture and reduce water usage.
  • Adaptability: Enables farming in regions with limited rainfall.
  • Cost-Effectiveness: Reduces the need for irrigation infrastructure and water costs.
