Soybean Cultivation

       
               
           

Soybean

           

Climate: Soybean thrives in warm and moist climates. The optimal temperature for growth is between 20°C to 30°C.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 60-80 kg per hectare.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and rich in organic matter. Plow the field thoroughly and ensure the soil is free of weeds. The ideal soil pH is between 6.0 and 7.0.

           

Planting: Sow seeds at a depth of 2.5-4 cm with a spacing of 45 cm between rows and 4-5 cm between plants. Soybean can be planted directly in the field.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer such as N:P:K (Nitrogen:Phosphorus:Potassium) in the ratio of 20:60:40 kg/ha. Soybean benefits from nitrogen-fixing bacteria, so inoculating seeds with Rhizobium can enhance growth.

           

Water Supply: Soybean requires adequate moisture, especially during flowering and pod formation. Irrigate the crop regularly but avoid waterlogging. The critical stages for water are germination, flowering, and pod filling.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include rust, blight, and root rot. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides. Ensure proper field sanitation and monitor for pest and disease outbreaks.
