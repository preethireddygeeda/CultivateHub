Carrot Cultivation

Carrot

Climate: Carrots thrive in cool to moderate climates, with optimal temperatures between 15°C to 20°C.

Seed Requirement: Approximately 1-2 kg of seeds per hectare.

Land Preparation: The soil should be loose, well-drained, and free of stones. Deep plowing is essential to ensure proper root development. Aim for a soil pH of 6.0 to 6.8.

Planting: Seeds are usually sown directly in the field in rows 30-40 cm apart. Seedlings should be thinned out to maintain proper spacing of 5-10 cm between plants.

Fertilizers: A balanced N:P:K fertilizer ratio of 100:50:50 kg/ha is recommended, applied at planting and as a side dressing during growth.

Water Supply: Carrots require consistent moisture, especially during germination and root development. Irrigate regularly but avoid waterlogging.

Disease Management: Common diseases include Alternaria leaf blight and root rot. Use disease-resistant varieties, rotate crops, and apply fungicides if necessary. Ensure proper field hygiene to minimize disease incidence.
