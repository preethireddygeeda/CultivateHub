Apple Cultivation

       
               
           

Apple

           

Climate: Apples thrive in temperate climates with cold winters and warm summers. Ideal temperatures range from -10°C to 30°C.

           

Seed Requirement: Approximately 1,000 to 2,000 seeds per hectare or grafted seedlings for orchard planting.

           

Land Preparation: The soil should be well-drained and rich in organic matter. Land should be plowed deeply and prepared in advance to ensure proper drainage.

           

Planting: Plant seedlings during the dormant season (late winter to early spring). Space plants 4-6 meters apart in rows for proper growth.

           

Fertilizers: Apply a balanced fertilizer with N:P:K ratio of 10:10:10 or similar, depending on soil tests. Fertilization should occur in early spring.

           

Water Supply: Regular watering is crucial, especially during flowering and fruit development. Maintain consistent soil moisture but avoid waterlogging.

           

Disease Management: Common diseases include apple scab, powdery mildew, and fire blight. Use disease-resistant varieties, practice crop rotation, and apply appropriate fungicides as necessary.
